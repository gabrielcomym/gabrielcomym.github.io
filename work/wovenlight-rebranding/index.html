WovenLight — Rebranding

Overview

WovenLight Rebranding was a brand evolution initiative focused on bringing consistency, trust and strategic clarity to a company that had already grown beyond the identity system it was using.

WovenLight is a hybrid investment and technology firm that uses data analytics and AI to maximize value creation across portfolio companies throughout the investment lifecycle. I joined in early 2024 as the company’s first Principal Designer, working across product leadership, design direction, branding and end-to-end design.

This project focused on evolving the brand so it could better support the company’s next stage of growth, improve consistency across touchpoints and present a more cohesive and credible image ahead of new investment opportunities.

Role and Scope

As Principal Designer at WovenLight, my broader responsibility included product leadership and vision, end-to-end product design, design direction and branding.

For this project, the scope included:

  • branding
  • design direction
  • stakeholder management
  • vendor management

The work also sat inside a wider leadership scope that included:

  • design leadership and vision in a 20-person startup
  • close collaboration with founders and senior leadership
  • alignment across internal teams and external contributors
  • creating more scalable design foundations for future growth

The Problem

In my first month at WovenLight, I conducted a design audit that revealed significant brand inconsistencies across products, presentations, social media and other touchpoints.

The existing brand assets and guidelines had been created by a contractor in 2019 and had not kept pace with the company’s growth.

This led to several issues:

  • fragmented brand expression across channels
  • inconsistent use of assets and visual language
  • weak alignment between brand and business maturity
  • limited clarity for internal and external implementation
  • reduced cohesion across products, communications and presentations

The brand no longer reflected the level of ambition or sophistication the company needed to project.

Why It Mattered

The project mattered because brand consistency was directly tied to trust, credibility and growth.

At that stage, WovenLight needed a visual identity that could:

  • align with its growth ambitions
  • support new investment opportunities
  • strengthen client and market perception
  • improve internal consistency across teams
  • create a more scalable foundation for future brand applications

This made the work more than a visual refresh. It was a strategic brand alignment project.

Discovery and Research

The project began with a comprehensive brand audit to identify inconsistencies across touchpoints.

This phase included:

  • auditing products and internal materials
  • auditing presentations and communication assets
  • reviewing social media and other public touchpoints
  • identifying inconsistencies in existing brand applications
  • documenting findings for leadership review

I then presented the findings to the Founder, CCO and COO, highlighting the main issues and proposing actionable steps to unify and modernize the brand.

This established a clear strategic foundation for the work that followed.

Planning and Alignment

An important part of the project was securing alignment with leadership before moving into visual exploration and delivery.

The planning and alignment work focused on:

  • clarifying the goals of the brand evolution
  • defining the scope of change
  • aligning expectations with the Founder’s direction
  • ensuring leadership visibility into the audit findings
  • setting a shared direction for a more modern and coherent brand

This step mattered because the work touched multiple touchpoints, stakeholders and external vendors, and needed strong internal alignment to be implemented consistently.

Key Insights

Several important insights emerged from the audit and exploration process:

  • the issue was not only inconsistency, but an overall lack of a scalable brand system
  • the relationship between the symbol and typeface needed refinement
  • the existing palette and visual balance did not fully convey the level of trust, luxury and modernity the company wanted to project
  • brand inconsistency was affecting both internal efficiency and external perception
  • the company needed a clearer system for application, not only updated aesthetics

These insights shaped the project into a full brand evolution rather than a superficial refresh.

Product and Design Decisions

The project translated audit findings and exploration into a set of clear brand decisions:

  • refine the relationship between symbol and typeface
  • optimize the color palette
  • improve visual balance and scalability
  • evolve the identity toward a more modern, cohesive and timeless direction
  • define clearer application rules across touchpoints
  • create a stronger system of assets, templates and guidelines
  • provide explicit dos and don'ts to reduce ambiguity in implementation

The work was also informed by:

  • competitor analysis
  • visual exploration
  • technical observations such as contrast ratios and scaling
  • color studies based on references that supported trust, luxury and modernity

Ideation and Validation

The exploration phase focused on testing and refining the right visual direction for the brand.

This work included:

  • competitor analysis
  • visual exploration
  • technical observations
  • color study

Rather than jumping directly to final assets, the process evaluated how different visual decisions affected:

  • contrast and readability
  • symbol-to-type relationships
  • scale and flexibility
  • overall balance
  • market perception

The evolving proposals were then socialized with leadership to ensure the new identity direction remained aligned with business expectations.

Delivery

In the delivery phase, I created the system needed to make the refreshed brand usable and consistent in practice.

This included:

  • updated brand guidelines
  • refreshed brand assets
  • application standards
  • a set of dos and don'ts
  • new presentation templates
  • collaboration on the company website redesign
  • design direction for third-party vendors
  • vendor management to keep implementation aligned with the refreshed brand

After presenting the deliverables to leadership, I secured full stakeholder approval and alignment on the updated brand identity.

End-to-End Process Summary

The full process can be summarized as:

  1. Audit the brand across products, communications and public touchpoints.
  2. Identify inconsistencies and structural weaknesses in the existing identity system.
  3. Present findings and opportunities to leadership.
  4. Align on the goals and scope of the brand evolution.
  5. Explore visual directions through competitor analysis, technical observations and color studies.
  6. Refine the identity system, including symbol, type and color relationships.
  7. Build updated guidelines, assets and templates.
  8. Extend the new brand direction across presentations, website work and vendor outputs.
  9. Secure stakeholder approval and implement the refreshed system.

Lessons from the Project

This case reinforces a few design lessons:

  • early brand audits can reveal structural issues that affect both perception and execution
  • a strong brand system needs rules, assets and implementation clarity, not only aesthetic improvements
  • stakeholder alignment is essential when brand work touches multiple teams and leadership priorities
  • brand evolution can create business value when it strengthens trust, internal consistency and readiness for growth
